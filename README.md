# HR_ANALYTICS
#📊 HR Analytics Dashboard – Employee Attrition Analysis

#🧠 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations, impacting productivity, cost, and team stability. This project analyzes employee data to uncover key factors driving attrition and provides actionable insights using data visualization.

The goal is to help HR teams make data-driven decisions to reduce employee turnover.

#🎯 Objectives

Identify key factors influencing employee attrition

Analyze trends across departments, job roles, and salary levels

Understand the impact of job satisfaction, overtime, and experience

Build an interactive dashboard for business insights

📁 Dataset

Source: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset

Records: 1470 employees

Features include:

Demographics (Age, Gender, Marital Status)

Job-related (Department, Job Role, Job Level)

Compensation (Monthly Income, Salary Hike)

Work factors (Overtime, Work-Life Balance, Job Satisfaction)

Experience (Years at Company, Years Since Last Promotion)

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

Python – Data cleaning & preprocessing

MySQL – Data storage & querying

Power BI – Dashboard creation & visualization

🔍 Key Insights

🔴 Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition

🔴 Low job satisfaction strongly correlates with employee exits

🔴 Sales department has the highest attrition rate

🔴 Employees with lower salary bands are more likely to leave

🔴 Lack of promotion over time increases attrition risk

This analysis helps organizations:

Improve employee retention strategies

Optimize compensation and promotion policies

Enhance work-life balance initiatives

Identify high-risk employee segments
